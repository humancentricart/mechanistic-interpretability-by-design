Presenters

Fabio Massimo Zanzotto

Full Professor, Human-centric ART, University of Rome Tor Vergata (Italy) and Senior Research Scientist, AlmaWave (Italy)

email: fabio.massimo.zanzotto@uniroma2.it ( Google Scholar , Scopus )

Research Interests: Since 1998, Fabio has been actively engaged in AI and in NLP research, focusing on ethical considerations, AI applications in tourism and healthcare, and fundamental AI concepts. He has coordinated several research projects, including MeMo and KATY. Fabio also oversees collaborations with companies in NLP, showcasing his significant impact on both academia and industry.

Expertise: Zanzotto's contributions include work on "traditional" NLP as well as neural network based NLP. Indeed, he has worked in automatic textual entailment recognition, syntactic parsing, and the application of distributed and distributional models to language syntax and semantics. He is currently working in understanding how traditional NLP is reflected in Tb-LLMs.

Teaching Experience: Fabio has more than 30 years of teaching experience. He has been the instructor of the NLP course at the University of Rome Tor Vergata since the academic year 2006-2007, and seems to have a captivating way of presenting complex information (see this X post from ACL 2025).

He delivered 4 tutorials during his career:

  1. F.M.Z., E.S. Ruzzetti, Understanding Memorization and Generalization in Transformer-based Large Language Models and beyond , IJCNN, 2025.
  2. F.M.Z., Textual Entailment Recognition for Web Based Question-Answering, in Web Intelligence 2012.
  3. Ido Dagan, Dan Roth, F.M.Z., Textual Entailment, in ACL 2007. This tutorial is now a book: Recognizing Textual Entailment: Models and Applications .
  4. R. Basili, F.M.Z., Parsing Robusto: Statistical and Symbolic Approaches for Large-Scale Syntactic Recognition , in AIIA, 2001.

Elena Sofia Ruzzetti

Postdoc, Human-centric ART, University of Rome Tor Vergata

email: elena.sofia.ruzzetti@uniroma2.it ( Google Scholar , Scopus )

Research Interests: Elena is interested in Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence. In particular, she focuses on the Interpretability of Deep Learning models for NLP. She also works in measuring and mitigating privacy risks in LLMs.

Expertise: She is the author of more than 20 publications in international and national forums. She actively serves the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL) community by acting as a reviewer in ARR for all the ACL conferences.

Teaching Experience: Ruzzetti has relevant experience in teaching NLP courses (in English), as she is involved in the course of Practical NLP at the University of Rome Tor Vergata as a formal duty in her Ph.D. studies; she also taught the Master's course Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity at the University of Rome Tor Vergata, covering privacy risks in machine learning and LLMs, robust training, machine unlearning, adversarial machine learning, and jailbreaking.

She delivered one tutorial during her career:

  1. F.M.Z., E.S. Ruzzetti, Understanding Memorization and Generalization in Transformer-based Large Language Models and beyond , IJCNN, 2025.

Leonardo Ranaldi

Postdoc, School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh, UK and Tenure Track at University of Rome Tor Vergata

email: leonardo.ranaldi@ed.ac.uk ( Google Scholar , Scopus )

Research Interests: Leonardo Ranaldi is a Research Associate at the University of Edinburgh and an Assistant Professor at the University of Rome Tor Vergata. His research focuses on multilingual generative reasoning, with experience in efficient, low-resource alignment, and neuro-symbolic reasoning. He is actively involved in pushing the boundaries of NLP and AI, exploring how to make models more robust, efficient, and interpretable across different languages and reasoning paradigms.

Expertise: He is the author of more than 50 publications in international and national forums. He is active on the senior committees of major conferences and has published in NLP/CL venues. He even plays a key role in the community by co-organising workshops and shared tasks, including the 3rd MathNLP, ORACLE, and SemEval 2026 Task11 .

Teaching Experience: Leonardo Ranaldi has relevant experience in teaching AI and CL courses. He is an Assistant Professor at the University of Rome Tor Vergata, where he teaches Computational Linguistics and ML, and has previously lectured on Natural Language Inference at the University of Edinburgh.
