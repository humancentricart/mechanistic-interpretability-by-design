Abstract

Transformer-based Large Language Models (Tb-LLMs) demonstrate extraordinary capabilities and, thus, change the approach of conducting research even in Knowledge Representation.

Building on existing research that neural networks (NNs) on discrete symbols operate as holistic reasoners over classical symbolic approaches, this tutorial aims to present the dominant paradigm of Tb-LLMs according to the idea that these models can be understood with neuro-symbolic, ontology-grounded approaches and controlled through mechanistic, interpretable, or explainable KG reasoning methods.

Offering a profound understanding of the inner workings of these models, the tutorial will help practitioners build novel, transparent architectures to model symbolic knowledge in NN.
